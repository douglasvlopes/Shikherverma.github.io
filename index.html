My name is Douglas Valeiro Lopes, I was born in 1994 in Sao Paulo, Brazil. Son of a designer I was always interested in drawings, paintings, movies, architecture, anything related to art. Always mesmerized by the importance of architecture in people’s life, and dreaming I could be part of that.

I did most of my primary education in Sao Paulo, except for three years in Ibiuna, a small town, before coming back to Sao Paulo when I was accepted in the Mackenzie Presbyterian University in São Paulo.

Accepted in Brazilian Government Program called Brazil Scientific Mobility Program which, students, from all around the country are selected through a performance academic analysis to receive the benefit of studying abroad for one year with full expense cover scholarship (starting on August 2015).

I was an exchange student at Washington University in St. Louis for two semester and now I am in Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago, participating of a Research In Digital Fabrication, oriented by professor Brett Balogh. I’ll publish everything I find interesting and inspiring, follow me.
